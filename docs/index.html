Description

Inspired by Advance Wars, Rochambeau Wars is a turn-based strategy game that pays homage to the GBA era. The three unit types the player encounters work in a rock-paper-scissors fashion, infantry are strong against anti-tank units, tanks are strong against infantry, and anti-tank units are strong against tanks. Play with a friend in 2-player mode or play against the AI and capture your opponent’s headquarters to win

Team Reflection

Our team was very effective in they way we planned out and divided work. It was intentionally done in a way so that no team member needed to wait for another team member to finish something. There was always something to be done. So, in this way, we avoided too many development dependencies that would have slowed production. You can see this in the development log earlier in this document. Because of this increased speed of development, we were able to accomplish a lot in the time given. We were also good at learning from each other and doing research when needed – whether it be learning how Unity does animations, or sound, etc. really anything that we needed to get the desired functionality out of Unity. The Unity Documentation was a big help for all of this.

If we had another two weeks to work on the project, really spending the time to fine tune the sound design and music we had in the works and getting it into the game would be the first things on the list. Some sound effects and music were created, but when added to the game, had a major negative impact on the way the game presented itself. Since we ran out of time, we couldn’t spend the time to really fine tune the sound element of the game, and as such, it wasn’t ready to be added. So, the top three priority items would be to finish creating and fine-tuning sound effects, finish creating and fine tuning the music, and then get those sounds added to the game. Additionally, we ran out of time to get the AI and therefore single player added. The AI was working but had bugs, leading us to decide that it was best to leave it out for the final class build. Getting the bugs worked out of the AI and getting the single player maps up and running would also be on the list.
