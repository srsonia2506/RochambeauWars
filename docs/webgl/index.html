Respondent 2:

  • "The game is still a work in progress. I did not know the rules of the game but figured it out. You can win by capturing the enemy capital or by killing all their units. I tried to check the field guide for information, but it turned up blank. I could not use the L key to back out of it and had to reload the page. The quit button also caused a similar issue. The gameplay is straight forward. The units have limited movement and strengths based on their surroundings. The mechanic is quite clever, especially when the enemy secures a location like a city, bolstering their defenses. Some units have clear strengths over others, like the AT vs the Tank. Positioning your units for their specific matchups is a great strategic element. One potential bug I found was that regardless of whether a unit was moved and the turn was ended, its energy/gasoline still went down. I recommend adding a field manual for more clarity and music/sound for more enjoyability. The game has good strategic components that make it like advanced chess. Another interesting concept would be if the units had different ranges, for instance, the tank could shoot furthest but would have the least mobility."

Decisions Reached:

  • As noted by respondents 1 and 2, the field manual needed to be added.
  • Some consider it a bug that units consume fuel when not moving. This was intentional, but we will look at how the game flows when this is not the case.
  • Once the field manual was added, it was successful, but players complained that they had to exit a match to read it. We will consider adding a way to view it during a match.
  • Respondent 3 makes a good point about mirroring the red player’s unit overlay, it will be done.
  • Respondent 5’s control scheme swap suggestion is good, and we will change the controls to K and L.
  • Some were indifferent to the lack of sound; others really want it. Sound should be added but mutable.
  • Some proposed new features, but due to time constraints and scope, these will likely not be added.
